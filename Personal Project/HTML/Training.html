Freestyle (Front Crawl)

Freestyle is the fastest swimming stroke, known for its alternating arm strokes and continuous flutter kick. Here's how to perform it:

  • Start by lying flat in the water, keeping your body streamlined.
  • Alternate your arms in a windmill motion, pulling them through the water in front of you.
  • Use a flutter kick to propel yourself forward, keeping your legs straight with a slight bend in the knees.
  • Keep your head in line with your spine and rotate it to breathe, taking a quick inhale as your head turns to the side.

Tip: Focus on maintaining a smooth, continuous motion to maximize speed and reduce drag.

Backstroke

The backstroke is performed on your back, using a flutter kick and alternating arm strokes. Here's how to do it:

  • Lie on your back with your body flat and your arms extended above your head.
  • Use a flutter kick to maintain momentum, with a gentle, steady movement of your legs.
  • Alternate your arms in a circular motion, keeping your elbows high and your hands entering the water above your shoulders.
  • Focus on breathing naturally, as your face remains above water.

Tip: Keep your core engaged to maintain a straight body position and reduce drag.

Breaststroke

The breaststroke is a slower stroke that involves a frog-like kick and a sweeping arm motion. Follow these steps:

  • Start with your arms extended in front of you and your body flat in the water.
  • Pull your arms outward in a circular motion, bringing them back in towards your chest.
  • Simultaneously perform a frog kick with your legs, pulling them together to push you forward.
  • Bring your arms forward as your legs come together, preparing to repeat the motion.

Tip: Focus on a long glide after each stroke to maximize the efficiency of the kick and arm movement.

Butterfly

The butterfly is a challenging but powerful stroke involving a dolphin-like kick and simultaneous arm movements. Here's how to do it:

  • Start with both arms extended in front of you and your body in a streamlined position.
  • Perform a dolphin kick, where both legs move together in a fluid up-and-down motion.
  • Pull your arms simultaneously in a circular motion, then push them forward together to repeat the stroke.
  • Ensure you breathe quickly as your arms come out of the water, keeping your head in line with your body.

Tip: Focus on body undulation and timing between the arm pull and the dolphin kick to avoid excessive fatigue.
